Design Philosophy

I consider a product's design its human interface. I focus on designing interactions that are delightful to the user, without compromising on build quality or practicality.

My goal is to create things that are beautiful, simple, and possible from an engineering & manufacturing perspective, and I can't wait to do that for your product.

Rocking chair

Comfort design

This chair was a study in making something beautiful and modern, but still comfortable. The zero gravity effect of sitting in this rocking chair is easy on the spine and lower back.

The rocking feature of this chair serves to calm nerves and even helps digestion. 9/10 doctors will fight for your right to rock on, it's true, I read it in a science book entitled "There's only one way to rock: Hard".

NABE vending bot

A concept for the future of food delivery and mobile vending

Taco trucks are out, it's time for bread robots to shine. Why shouldn't your croissant grow legs and deliver itself?

I undertook this project in order to design a new future for bread, one where the user (and their preferences as far as gluten) came first. This robot is a first-of-its kind delivery system that knows how to determine if you're celiac and will stuff the correct bread roll directly in your mouth, steal your wallet, and charge your card accordingly. Wallets, of course, are returned, but not before we scan your ID and sign you up for the Nabe bread bot newsletter.
